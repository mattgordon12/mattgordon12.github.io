Privacy Policy for Pulse
Last Updated: February 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Pulse ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and discloses information when you use our mobile application.

1. Information Collection and Use
To provide a functional and optimized gaming experience, we collect the following:

Local Game Data (AsyncStorage): We use local storage on your device to save game progress, high scores, and user settings. This data remains on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

Device Identifiers: We collect unique device identifiers (such as Android Advertising ID or Apple IDFA) to facilitate personalized advertising and analytics.

Transaction Information: When you make an In-App Purchase, the transaction is processed by the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. We do not collect or store your credit card numbers or billing addresses. We only receive a transaction confirmation (receipt) to unlock your purchased content.

2. Third-Party Services: Google AdMob
We use Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may collect and use data to show ads that are more relevant to you. This data includes:

Device ID and IP address.

Approximate location data.

User interaction with ads.
For more information, please review the Google Privacy & Terms.

3. Data Protection Rights (GDPR & CCPA)
GDPR (European Economic Area):
If you are a resident of the EEA, we only process personalized ads after obtaining your explicit consent via a Consent Management Platform (CMP). You have the right to access, correct, or delete your data at any time through your device settings.

CCPA (California):
California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected and to opt-out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. Sharing Device IDs for personalized ads may be considered "sharing" under CCPA. You can opt-out by enabling "Limit Ad Tracking" in your iOS or Android system settings.

4. Children’s Privacy
Pulse does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13 (or 16 in certain jurisdictions). If you are a parent and believe we have collected such data, please contact us.

5. Changes to This Policy
We may update our Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.
